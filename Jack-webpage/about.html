Jack Draper's Tennis Journey:
The Rising British Star

Early Life & Background

Jack Alexander Draper was born on December 22, 2001, in Sutton, London, and raised in Ashtead, Surrey. Tennis runs in his family - his father Roger Draper was the former chief executive of Sport England and the Lawn Tennis Association, while his mother Nicky Draper was a former junior British tennis champion and coach who first trained Jack when he started at age 6.

Unique Playing Style

Draper's playing style is characterized by an aggressive all-around game and strong serve. Despite being naturally right-handed, he plays left-handed, which essentially makes his backhand a second forehand. His forehand has been compared to Nadal's in its topspin, pace, and consistency. He has a great ability to mix up his serve, using slices and flat serves to keep opponents guessing on the return.

His "can-opener" serve creates particularly difficult angles for opponents, and his forehand combines power with a unique mix of top and side-spin that makes the ball drift sideways at the last instant, catching opponents off-guard.

Major Career Milestones

2024 - Breakthrough Year:

  • Won his first ATP title at the Stuttgart Open, becoming British men's No. 1
  • Claimed his first ATP 500 title at the Vienna Open, defeating Karen Khachanov in the final
  • Reached his first Grand Slam semifinal at the 2024 US Open
  • Became the youngest British No. 1 since Andy Murray at the same age in 2009

2025 - Continued Success:

  • Won his first Masters 1000 title at the 2025 Indian Wells Open
  • Reached the final of the Madrid Open, becoming only the second Brit after Andy Murray to make that final
  • Currently ranked world No. 5 and moving up to world No. 4 as his career-high

The nickname "The Star in the Making" perfectly captures Jack Draper's trajectory - from a promising junior with tennis in his DNA to Britain's new No. 1 and a legitimate contender for major titles. His unique left-handed style, combined with his powerful serve and Nadal-esque forehand, makes him one of the most exciting young talents in tennis today.
